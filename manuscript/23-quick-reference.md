# The Mother's Code — Quick Reference

Twenty-one principles, distilled. Come back to this page whenever you need the whole book in a few minutes.

1. **The Belief Effect** — Treat effort as worth investing in. Your belief becomes their self-belief.
2. **The Words That Build** — Praise process and effort, not fixed traits. Add "yet" to every "I can't."
3. **The Safety Behind the Courage** — Security produces courage, not caution. Repair matters more than perfection.
4. **Letting Them Fall** — Struggle, in the right dose, is a form of love. Only intervene when it's dangerous, not just uncomfortable.
5. **Presence Over Perfection** — Quality of attention beats quantity of hours. Fifteen undistracted minutes outweigh a distracted afternoon.
6. **The Mirror Effect** — Children absorb what they witness, not what they're told. Model regulation and accountability out loud.
7. **Purpose Before Pressure** — Connect achievement to internal meaning, not external validation. Autonomy sustains motivation; pressure erodes it.
8. **The Ripple** — Patterns pass down generations by default. Awareness is what lets you choose, not repeat.
9. **The Repair Reflex** — Ruptures are normal; reliable repair is what builds security. Return quickly, name it simply.
10. **Naming the Storm** — Labeling a feeling calms it. Name the emotion before you try to fix the problem.
11. **The Comparison Trap** — Children feel differential treatment even when it's unintentional. Catch comparison language before it becomes identity.
12. **The Gratitude Habit** — Structured noticing of good things, especially people, builds lasting wellbeing. Make it a daily routine.
13. **Small Hands, Real Work** — Early, real contribution builds lifelong competence. Say yes to slow, messy help before age five.
14. **The Screen in the Room** — Protect the moments your child is actively reaching for you. Presence beats total screen time.
15. **Waiting Well** — Delayed gratification is a taught strategy, not a fixed trait. Teach concrete ways to make waiting easier.
16. **The Question Behind the Question** — Curiosity fades when questions are dismissed. Meet "why" with wonder, not "just because."
17. **Touch That Tells the Truth** — Affectionate touch regulates stress at every age. Adapt its form as your child grows, don't retire it.
18. **The Rhythm of Rest** — Predictable routines build a felt sense of security. Protect the sequence more than the specific content.
19. **The Discipline Shift** — Teaching-oriented discipline outperforms punishment. Connect consequences logically; explain the why.
20. **The Long Goodbye** — Autonomy-granting is the adolescent version of a secure base. Practice small independence before the big ones arrive.
21. **The Village Effect** — You were never meant to do this alone. Nurture the trusted adults already around your child.
